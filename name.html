CHINESE HUNDRED SURNAMES (Family Names)

"HUNDRED SURNAMES" has a history of more than 1,000 years and has been widely spread in China since the Northern Song Dynasty in the 10th century. "HUNDRED SURNAMES" uses four-character style to arrange the surnames, and the rhymes in sentences have played a huge role in the inheritance of Chinese surname culture and the understanding of Chinese characters. "HUNDRED SURNAMES" is named "Hundred", because the ancients often used "Hundred" as an empty word to indicate "many". In addition, this book has 411 surnames, and later increased to 504, including 444 single surnames and 60 complex surnames.

STORIES OF HUNDRED CHINESE SURNAMES

Zhao
赵

In ancient times, a man drove for King Zhou Mu, who gave the Zhao city to him, and his descendants took the city name as the surname. In addition, Zhao Kuangyin was the founding emperor of the Song Dynasty, and it is said that the surname Zhao is the first in “HUNDRED SURNAMES” to be related to this.

Qian
钱

Qian means money in Chinese. Peng Zu's grandson Peng Fu served as sergeant in the court of theWestern Zhou Dynasty, and his descendants used his official name as his surname.

Sun
孙

One descendant of King Zhou Wen is called Hui Sun, and his descendants use his first name "Sun" as the surname.

Li
李

One Li Tao's descendant were punished for offending King Zhou(A stunner). His wife and children picked fruits under a plum tree to fill their hunger and survived. Later, his descendants changed their surname to "Lee" in honor of this Li Tao's descendant.

Zhou
周

According to legend, there was a general named Zhou Chang in ancient, and there was another officer named Zhou Ren in the Shang Dynasty. The descendants of these two persons all used Zhou as their surname, and some descendants of King Zhou Wen or Zhou Gong also used Zhou asSurname, and in the Tang Dynasty to avoid Tang Xuanzong's (the Emperor of Tang Dynasty) name,Ji’ descendants took use of Zhou as Surname instead.

Wu
吴

The sons of Zhou Taiwang (an ancient Emperor), established the Wu Kingdom, and their descendants are called Wu family, than used Wu as their surname.

Zheng
郑

The descendants of king Zheng Huang (an ancient Emperor) is named by his country.

Xǔ
许

The descendants of ancient Yan Emperor. Shu Wen established Xu country, and future generations took Xu as their surname.

Huáng
黄

The Huang surname mainly originated from surname of the Ying and minority ethnic group. It originated in northern China. It later moved to the Central Plains and established the HuangKingdom.

Liao
廖

According to legend, Emperor Xun had a descendant called Shu'an. During the Xia Dynasty, he was called Liao Shu'an because he was sealed in Liao country, and his descendants took thecountry’s name as the Liao.
